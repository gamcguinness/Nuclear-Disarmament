World War 2

The Cold War

Proliferation

The first nuclear weapon was developed in 1945 by the United States and used against Japan in the same year. This marked a turning point in military history and had a profound impact on geopolitics. The devastation caused by the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki demonstrated the immense destructive power of nuclear weapons and raised concerns about the domestic security of countries around the globe. This led to the first arms race between the US and Russia.

The cold war was a period of intense competition between the United States and the Soviet Union in the 1980s to develop and deploy increasingly sophisticated nuclear weapons and delivery systems. This arms race was fuelled by mutual mistrust, fear, and a desire to maintain a strategic advantage over the other superpower. The competition caused enormous innovation and mass production of these weapons. This dramatically increased the threat of these weapons and introduced them as a key factor in geopolitics.

After the Cold War, both the United States and the Soviet Union played a key role in proliferating nuclear weapons to their allies and client states. For example, the United States provided nuclear weapons technology to the United Kingdom, France, and Israel, while the Soviet Union provided nuclear weapons assistance to China and North Korea. Other countries, including the United Kingdom, France, and China, also developed their own nuclear weapons programs in response to the perceived threat from the superpowers in the Cold War. This led to a global proliferation of nuclear weapons, with many countries viewing them as a means of ensuring their own security and deterring potential adversaries.
